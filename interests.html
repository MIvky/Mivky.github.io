My Interests

  1. Reading and literature

    2. "A reader lives a thousand lives before he dies . . . The man who never reads lives only one."
    George R.R. Martin

    I think there's not much to add here. I love reading, from fictional books to biographies; from great epic works to short stories. In a sea of information we gain from looking on our screens, I believe it to be neccessary to shut out all mental diversions and plunge into a solid text, that's a window in an another persons' thinking. It broadens your perspective and your knowledge. Hell, somewhere, someone probably went what we're going through. Maybe they'll give us some kind of answer.

  2. Music

    3. "Do it again on the next verse and people think you meant it."
    Chet Atkins

    If it's not clear from my CV and my current job, music is a big part of me. Since I was a small lad I was involved with a lot of choirs and ansambles. Music gave me the option to travel, to meet other people from all walks of life. Because of this little interes, I've traveled to France, Germany, Poland, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Ireland, Chille, Italy, Montenegro, Serbia, Bulgaria, Slovakia. I've performed in front of more than 50.000 people, and I've performed in more intimate occassions. Nevertheles, music gave me a livelihood, and the opportunity to broaden my perspective.

  3. Travel

    4. "Not all those who wander are lost"
    J.R.R. Tolkien

    I don't love traveling, per se, but I love new countries, new cultures, new mentalities. Much of my travels came thorugh my involvement in music. I love the small differences in culture between two countries. Those differences can be a solid foothold for a potential problem, but it can be a solution. Not for nothing, traveling gives us the ability to feel, breathe, taste and hear the local customs, enriched by tradition, and it can give us the ability to see our own country with a new set of glasses.

  4. Cooking

    5. I like eating, cooking and shopping. It all goes together.
    Kyle MacLachlan

    I've yet to meet a person who doesn't love to eat. Cooking, on the other hand is a different thing. Some people don't hate it, some people do, but I love it. And I don't love the quick and snappy recipes that are easy. I love the multy-hour outdoor cooking, like barbecue, or bean stew; you know, the things that take up most of your weekend. That casual 20 minute stir-fry doesn't interest me, no sir. I like them looking in the open fire with a beer in my hand and a possy of my friends telling me how I should do it.
